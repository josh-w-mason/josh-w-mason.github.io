2. A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

Often strengths and limitations tend to be flipsides of the same coin. I think one of my big strengths, which is a trait I’ve had since I can remember, is being an enthusiastic auto-didact - I am totally comfortable going out of my way to find out new things and trying stuff out to see what happens. I love learning by doing, i.e “what does this button do?” and getting feedback in real time. I'm big on praxis. The limitation of this is that I can sometimes go down rabbit holes or get overly fixated on trying to solve some minor problem to the detriment of all else. Sometimes I need to remind myself of the bigger picture, or else take a break and refresh my mind. This is essentially an offshoot of perfectionism. Occasionally I can get a bit of imposter syndrome too, and forget to remind myself to be proud of what i DO know or have already accomplished.

4. A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:
- An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives
- What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

When working collaboratively I think it’s important to ensure you give others space and be a good listener. And be transparent with what you are up to and where you’re at, too. Don’t talk over others, criticize arbitrarily or non constructively, it’s important to be open minded. Ask questions if something initially seems weird or confusing. From a personal standpoint, managing my time means macro and micro - what needs to be done by the end of the sprint? How many hours per day? How am I tracking for each specific task? Keep to a regular schedule, but one that has a bit of give and take (for example, it’s good to get in fresh air or a walk in every day, but when you do this can change depending on weather). Also, BEWARE THE PLANNING FALLACY. Always add 10+% to your estimate for when things inevitably go a bit off course, or when there are tech difficulties. Accumulate a bit of surplus productivity capital at the start of the week, so that if I'm feeling unwell or something comes up I have a bit of runway I can use and not panic too much.

When things feel like they are getting to me, take a small break. Listen to some comforting music or chill ASMR. Do some mindfulness on the Waking Up app. Play guitar / piano to get brain in different mode. Eat something! Drink some water. Walk around the block. If its a major situation and these aren’t working, reach out to the EDA staff and explain where I’m at.
