1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

When I left a band in late 2011 that I had been playing in for over three years. I had to weigh up the interests of the group vs my own. They were all really great people, I loved the music and did have a great time playing shows across the country, but ultimately I was wanting to devote more time to exploring and creating my own music, which was a radically different genre. That plus some of the people in the scene the band would occassionally hang out with would rub me the wrong way, in quite an explicit clash of values (extreme pessimism plus some proto fascism sprinkled in there). The group was able to replace me fairly easily and we all stayed on good terms, and even though creatively I was more on my own going forward, I felt this to be more liberating and ended up being happier and learning a lot more.


2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity

As a quick aside, I find it tricky being very specific about identity - like if the answer is supposed to be "I identify as [x, y, z groups/isms/labels]" I'm not usually sure how to answer. Categories such as gender, ethnicity, religion etc don't really hold much sway for me, or I've not felt the pull to conform to the stereotypes of any of these classes anyway (cue Humanities' professors going "aha - of course you don't notice the privilege-water you are swimming in!" to which I would reply "we don't have time to get into that stuff in a Dev Academy post") ) The long ish version: I’m one of those ahistorical millennials growing up in the era of newly and unprecedentedly unleashed First World secular capitalist anomie, on the cusp of the internet age. My mother was (and kinda still is) of the Ba’hai faith, which has a core acceptance of all the worlds religions. Dad is Apatheist at best. Middle class, white, gratuitously compassionate and hippie-adjacent. Great respect for music, the arts and the numinous in general, rather than through the lens of any particular intellectual tradition. Curious about the human condition. My peers, aka the people that stuck around me and ended up becoming my friend group are all very intellectual, generous, funny, liberal types. 5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why

In a previous job, one particular co-worker would give myself and the other new-ish person there a hard time whenever we were working on tasks. This one particular task involved driving up and down the rows cutting out the dead plants and I think I may have missed the odd one, or the other newbie missed one; it was hard to know for sure while working on different areas at the same time. Anyway, this guy who had been there a while and thought he could basically talk to people however he wanted kept yelling and threatening us saying the boss would fire us etc (it was actually a pretty toxic system because it seemed like there was vicarious responsibility, so it was probably more him getting mad because it may reflect badly on him). I just calmly said something like “hey I don’t know for sure if I’m the one responsible, but I hear you and I can always go back and double check if it’s an issue. I’ll be as vigilant as I can but still its possible some might slip through the cracks seeing as I’m still a newbie”.

To be honest he didn’t really care or acknowledge what I said, and ultimately he was one of the reasons I ended up leaving the company slightly earlier than planned. If I had to stay and ended up in a similar situation again, I would be more assertive and say “first of all I don't appreciate being spoken to like that, and it’s not helping the situation”. Then if it continued I would explain the situation to the higher ups and see if they would have a word. Hopefully an understanding is reached, or compromise (e.g we might just not work well together, and therefore get put on different tasks going forward). If THAT'S not possible and work is just constant mental anguish I would probably still leave. Generally speaking, not much is worth sacrificing your long-term mental wellbeing for.
